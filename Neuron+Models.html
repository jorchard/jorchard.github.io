Hodgkin-Huxley Model¶

In [2]:
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pylab as plt
%matplotlib inline

Let:

  • $V$ be voltage (membrane potential),
  • $J_\mathrm{M}$ is the total current across the membrane
  • $V_\mathrm{L}$, $V_\mathrm{Na}$, $V_\mathrm{K}$ zero-current potentials for leak, sodium, and potassium
  • $g_\mathrm{L}$, $g_\mathrm{Na}$, $g_\mathrm{K}$ conductance for leak, sodium, and potassium channels
  • $n^4$ fraction of potassium channels open
  • $m^3 h$ fraction of sodium channels open
  • $n_\infty$, $m_\infty$, and $h_\infty$ are equilibrium solutions for $n$, $m$, and $h$, respectively.
  • $c$, $\tau_n$, $\tau_m$, $\tau_h$ are time constants

\begin{align} c \frac{dV}{dt} &= J_\mathrm{M} - g_\mathrm{L} \left( V - V_\mathrm{L} \right) - g_\mathrm{Na} m^3 h \left( V - V_\mathrm{NA} \right) - g_\mathrm{K} n^4 \left(V - V_\mathrm{K} \right) \\ \tau_n \frac{dn}{dt} &= n_\infty - n \\ \tau_m \frac{dm}{dt} &= m_\infty - m \\ \tau_h \frac{dh}{dt} &= h_\infty - h \end{align}

Leaky Integrate-and-Fire Neuron¶

Introduction¶

Similar to HH model, but simpler. The LIF model does not model the spiking behaviour, just the sub-threshold voltage. When the membrane potential reaches the threshold, we record a spike (as a time stamp).

The sub-threshold membrane potential is governed by the DE, $\def\L{\mathrm{L}}$ $$ c \frac{dV}{dt} = J_\mathrm{in} - g_\L (V -V_\L) \ , \quad V<V_\L $$ where $c$ is the membrane capacitance, $J_\mathrm{in}$ is the membrane current, $g_\L$ is the leak conductance, and $V_\L$ is the zero-current potential.

Using the change of variables, $v = \frac{V - V_\L}{V_\mathrm{th} - V_\L}$, where $V_\mathrm{th}$ is the voltage threshold for spiking, and Ohm's Law ($V = JR$), our DE can be written $$ \tau_m \frac{dv}{dt} = v_\mathrm{in}(t) - v $$ where $\tau_m$ is the membrane time constant, and $v_\mathrm{in}$ is the input voltage (current).

def G(vin):
    tau_ref = 0.002
    tau_m = 0.02
    if vin>1.:
        return 1./(tau_ref - tau_m*np.log(1.-1./vin))
    else:
        return 0.

Tuning Curves / Activation Functions¶

Logistic¶

Given input $z$, the activity of the sigmoid neuron is $\sigma (z)$, where $\sigma$ is

$$ \sigma (z) = \frac{1}{1 + \exp (-z)} $$

In [70]:
def logistic(z):
    return 1./(1+np.exp(-z))
